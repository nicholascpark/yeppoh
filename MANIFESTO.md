# Manifesto

Yeppoh is a generative-biology art practice.

The work is a population of soft-body creatures whose morphology and behavior emerge through multi-agent reinforcement learning against simulated physics. Each creature is one run: one seed, one checkpoint, one environment, one unrepeatable trajectory from initialization to maturity. Nothing is re-rolled. Nothing is refined. The creature is captured, and the capture is the work.

This is a rebuke to prompt-in, image-out. Form is not specified here. Form is conditioned. What the artist authors is the pipeline, the environment, the physics — and then the discipline to let emergence run. Selection comes only at the end: which creature, at which moment, in which medium, becomes presented work.

We call this emergence as craft. It is slow. It cannot be prompted. It cannot be re-requested. The creature that stabilizes at hour forty of training is not the creature that stabilized at hour four; they are different lives. Collectors receive a specific life.

The name 예뿌 is a Korean baby-register word for pretty — a word used only for something small, living, and briefly here. The practice sits in a particular aesthetic inheritance: 간결함 (simplicity), 여백 (negative space), 한 (longing). Restraint over abundance. Weight over noise.

Every drop is permanent and additive. Nothing is retracted, re-minted, or disavowed. Early creatures will one day look less refined than later creatures. That is the point. The body of work is an argument evolving in public; collectors buy the trajectory, not the piece.

The medium will move. Today: light-field displays and resin sculpture. Tomorrow: creatures in your room. Eventually: substrates that do not yet have names. Never first-mover. Never last-mover. The practice arrives as the second wave with superior craft.

Each creature lives once. The practice has no v2.

—Yeppoh
